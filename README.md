# How to Write Well

**A cross-grade writing framework for Deutsche Schule Prag students in Grades 5–12.**

A single-page interactive guide built around one principle: the same eight disciplines are revisited every year, at increasing sophistication. Students at any stage can use the same tool — the framework scales to meet them.

🔗 [https://justinsteinmetz.github.io/HOW_TO_WRITE_WELL/](https://justinsteinmetz.github.io/HOW_TO_WRITE_WELL/)

---

## What it is

A self-contained study guide and reference tool. No teacher setup required. Students open it independently before, during, or after a writing task.

It is not a lesson. It is a framework students return to at every stage of their writing education.

---

## Structure

The guide is organised into six sections, accessible via the top navigation bar:

| Section | What it contains |
|---|---|
| Framework | Five stages of mastery — expandable cards showing how expectations develop from primary to graduation |
| Skills | Eight core disciplines — each one shown across all five stages |
| Checklist | A pre-submission checklist students tick before handing in work |
| Rubric | A self-assessment and peer-feedback rubric, differentiated by stage |
| Phrases | A bank of transition and linking phrases — tap to copy, adapt before use |
| Practice | Timed revision activities with a built-in countdown timer |

---

## The five stages

| Stage | Label | Approximate level |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 | Sentence Builder | Primary |
| Stage 2 | Paragraph Writer | Upper Primary |
| Stage 3 | Structured Writer | Lower Secondary (Grades 7–9) |
| Stage 4 | Academic Writer | Upper Secondary (Grades 10–12) |
| Stage 5 | Professional Stylist | Graduation+ |

The default view opens at Stage 1. Students select their stage using the tab strip at the top of the page.

---

## The eight writing disciplines

Each discipline is presented at all five stages. Students select a discipline to see how it develops:

1. Ideas & Planning
2. Sentence Craft
3. Paragraph Structure
4. Argument
5. Clarity
6. Style & Voice
7. Editing
8. Grammar

---

## Classroom use

**As a pre-writing reference** — Direct students to open the guide before starting a task. They select their stage, read the relevant discipline, and use the checklist before submitting.

**As a self-assessment tool** — After completing a draft, students use the rubric to assess their own writing against stage-appropriate criteria.

**As a peer-feedback tool** — The rubric is structured for peer use. Students mark Beginning / Developing / Proficient / Excellent against each criterion.

**As a revision prompt** — The timed activities section provides focused revision tasks. The built-in timer (2 min, 5 min, 10 min, 15 min, 30 min) supports structured independent practice.

---

## Differentiation

The framework is designed to be used simultaneously across a mixed-ability class. Students work within their own stage without the tool exposing that differentiation visually. A Grade 7 student using Stage 2 and a Grade 7 student using Stage 4 are looking at the same interface — only the content differs.
